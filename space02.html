Astronomers have found a planet in the 13 times that of Jupiter

We know that Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system. But astronomers using the Subaru telescope, discovered a massive planet, the name Super-Jupiter, which is located at a distance of 170 light years from Earth and orbits a star called Carr Andromeda.

How do astronomers report newly discovered planet is 13 times larger than Jupiter. Thanks to modern technology was able to get direct pictures of the planet, which is quite rare. Super-Jupiter orbits the star Carr Andromeda, which is 2.5 times greater than our sun. Astronomers believe that the age of a star system is 20-30 million years old, which is not very large by the standards of measure of the universe, writes space.com.

Due to such a large size of the planet, astronomers have placed it within the classification system between giant planets and stars, called brown dwarfs. Planet maintains a constant temperature of 1427 degrees Celsius.

Scientists say that, thanks to the pictures they drew near to getting an answer to the question of the dependence of the rate of formation of planetary systems from the mass of the star, located in the center of the system.

"These data allow us to understand the patterns of occurrence of planetary systems, which are much larger than our solar system," - said astronomer Phil Plait.
