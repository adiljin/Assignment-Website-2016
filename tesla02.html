Update Tesla allows cars to park yourself

Some cars Tesla Motors will be able to park on their own, without the driver inside, thanks to a software update that has become available over the weekend. The update also limits the speed of the semi-autonomous mode autopilot Tesla and introduces a number of enhancements, including automatic deceleration when driving around a curve and keeping the car on its lane, even if erased markings.

Director General of Tesla Elon Musk said the parking function - is a "baby step" towards its ultimate goal: to allow drivers to encourage self-managed and self-charging cars from anywhere using your phone.

"I really believe, perhaps with excessive optimism, that within two years, you will be able to bring your car across the country - said Musk in the conference. - This is the first small step in this direction. "

Currently, however, no system is completely autonomous.

"It is more parking on the remote control," - says Musk.

Owners should be in line with the Model S sedan or SUV Model X 10 meters from the place where you want to enter or leave that. Then, they must be within three meters of a car park and tell yourself using keychain or smartphone. The car can also leave this place when the driver of his call. If he went to the home garage, the car can also open and close the garage door.

Tesla says that this system is useful for places where it is difficult to park, but warns that it should only be used on private property, so as not yet discovered all the possible obstacles. The car can hit the bikes hanging from the ceiling of the garage, for example.

The software update also sets a new speed limit of the semi-autonomous Tesla mode. The car on autopilot (Autopilot) will now go to the limited speed on the roads of the residential sector, and on the road without a central divider. If the vehicle gets into a region with an autopilot mode, it will automatically slow down.

Musk said he was not aware of the emergency cases where Tesla worked on autopilot, but believes that this change will not be a problem for the owners.

"On the road without a central divider, where there is potential for serious collisions, it makes sense to reduce the rate of more than ten kilometers per hour," - he said.

Updates will affect about 60 000 vehicles, including sedans Model S, released after September 2014, and the new SUV Model X.
