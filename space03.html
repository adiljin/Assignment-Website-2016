The tail of black hole for the first time opened to scientists

Scientists have long been studying black holes, but now they were able to detect a black hole, we turn to the "tail", the emission of which can be examined with the help of modern telescopes.

Astronomers today known two types of black holes. The first type - is ordinary black holes, caused by the collapse of stars, and the second are the supermassive hole, located in the center of galaxies. All black holes are capable of absorbing energy and throw it in the form of jets - heated plasma beams moving at near-light speed. Some scientists believe that the black holes of different types should differ in their properties, according sci-news.com.

Researchers from the European Southern Observatory in Garching (Germany) reviewed in detail the radio spectrum jet, we studied its properties and found it "overclocked" hydrogen ions. These studies were conducted at the facility 4U 1630-47, which consists of a black hole and rotating around a big star, whose matter is gradually "fall" on the accretion disk.

"Interestingly, we found the line where they were supposed to be, but we saw that they were significantly shifted," said study co-author Dr. James Miller (Dr James Miller).

Researchers analyzed in detail the range of the jet ejected black hole, and found traces of hydrogen ion dispersed therein. Earlier this effect doubted many astronomers. It is also worth noting that during the observation of a black hole structure has changed dramatically. This is due to the fact that the companion star loses matter, feeding the black hole.

According to established theory, black holes suck in everything that falls into the range of their powers, including the stars and planets, but the researchers found that a supermassive black hole of our Milky Way is very bad "learns" it absorbed matter. Almost 99 per cent of the gas it emits back into space. From this it follows that only one percent of all matter falling into the attraction zone dominance black hole event horizon falls over. This discovery not only allows you to lift the curtain on the secrets of some of the stereotypes of models, but also explains the relative dimness of the core of many galaxies of the universe.
