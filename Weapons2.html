This NES Gun Is Actually a Real Modified Glock

BEFORE
AFTER

There are plenty of gun controllers on the market, but if you're looking for something with a bit more firepower, then this is the one for you.

Pictures of an actual working gun based on the NES Zapper used in Nintendo's Duck Hunt have been revealed online, and it looks scarily identical to the original. The weapon is actually a modified Glock based on the classic gaming accessory, so you definitely wouldn't want to use this with your Nintendo.

Images of the gun were posted to the official Facebook page of Precision Syndicate LLC, a machine prototyping machine company from Texas.

The company added the caption: "Finally... We were waiting for someone to let us do this. We weren't the first to do it but WOW, this Glock turned out good!"

The NES Zapper revolutionized the way gamers could play arcade shooters when it was released in 1984. It was first made available with the NES in Japan, before being released in the US and the rest of the world.
